# Werner: Une Théorie des Constructions Inductives (1994)

## Citation
Werner, B. "Une Théorie des Constructions Inductives." PhD thesis,
Université Paris VII, 1994.

## Key Contributions
- First complete proof of strong normalization of CIC (with strong elimination)
- Also considers eta-reductions in the type conversion rule
- Meta-theory of the Calculus of Inductive Constructions
- Strong normalization entails logical consistency and decidability of typing

## Sets in Types, Types in Sets (1997)
Werner also showed two mutual encodings of CIC in Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory:
- The number of universes in the type theory corresponds to the number of
  inaccessible cardinals in the set theory
- Both hierarchies of logical formalisms interleave with respect to expressive power
- Remarkably simple set-theoretical model of CIC using proof-irrelevance interpretation
