# Calculus of Constructions: Primary Sources and Bibliography

## Seminal Papers

1. **Coquand, T. (1985).** *Une Théorie des Constructions.* PhD thesis, University of Paris VII. Defended January 31, 1985.

2. **Coquand, T. and Huet, G. (1985).** "Constructions: A Higher Order Proof System for Mechanizing Mathematics." In *Proceedings of EUROCAL '85*, Linz, Austria. Springer LNCS 203, pp. 151–184.

3. **Coquand, T. and Huet, G. (1988).** "The Calculus of Constructions." *Information and Computation*, 76(2-3):95–120. DOI: 10.1016/0890-5401(88)90005-3.

4. **Coquand, T. (1990).** "Metamathematical Investigations of a Calculus of Constructions." In Odifreddi (ed.), *Logic and Computer Science*, Academic Press. Originally circulated privately in February 1987.

## Strong Normalization Proofs

5. **Coquand, T. and Gallier, J.H. (1990).** "A Proof of Strong Normalization for the Theory of Constructions Using a Kripke-Like Interpretation."

6. **Geuvers, H. and Nederhof, M.J. (1991).** "A Modular Proof of Strong Normalization for the Calculus of Constructions." *Journal of Functional Programming*, 1(2):155–189.

7. **Barras, B. and Werner, B. (1997).** "A Short and Flexible Proof of Strong Normalization for the Calculus of Constructions." In *Types for Proofs and Programs*, Springer LNCS 1158, pp. 44–60.

8. **Casinghino, C. (2022).** "Strong Normalization for the Calculus of Constructions." arXiv:2210.11240.

## Lambda Cube and Pure Type Systems

9. **Barendregt, H. (1991).** "Introduction to Generalized Type Systems." *Journal of Functional Programming*, 1(2):125–154.

10. **Barendregt, H. (1992).** *Lambda Calculi with Types.* In Abramsky, Gabbay, Maibaum (eds.), *Handbook of Logic in Computer Science*, Vol. 2, Oxford University Press.

## Curry-Howard Correspondence and Higher-Order Logic

11. **Geuvers, H. (1993).** "The Calculus of Constructions and Higher Order Logic." Technical Report, Faculty of Mathematics and Informatics, Catholic University Nijmegen.

12. **Geuvers, H. (1995).** "On the Adequacy of Representing Higher Order Intuitionistic Logic as a Pure Type System." *Theoretical Computer Science*, 150(2):335–357.

## Models and Semantics

13. **Hyland, M. and Pitts, A. (1989).** "The Theory of Constructions: Categorical Semantics and Topos-Theoretic Models." In *Categories in Computer Science and Logic*, AMS Contemporary Mathematics 92.

14. **Aczel, P. (1998).** "A Simple Model Construction for the Calculus of Constructions." In *Category Theory and Computer Science*, Springer LNCS 389.

15. **Werner, B. (1997).** "The Not So Simple Proof-Irrelevant Model of CC." In *Types for Proofs and Programs*, Springer LNCS 1512.

## Extensions and Inductive Constructions

16. **Paulin-Mohring, C. (1993).** "Inductive Definitions in the System Coq: Rules and Properties." In *Typed Lambda Calculi and Applications*, Springer LNCS 664.

17. **Pfenning, F. and Paulin-Mohring, C. (1989).** "Inductively Defined Types in the Calculus of Constructions." In *Mathematical Foundations of Programming Semantics*, Springer LNCS 442.

19. **Werner, B. (1998).** "The Relevance of Proof-Irrelevance." In *Workshop on Types and Computation*.
