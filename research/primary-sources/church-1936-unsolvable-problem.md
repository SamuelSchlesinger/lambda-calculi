# Church 1936 -- "An Unsolvable Problem of Elementary Number Theory"

## Citation

Church, A. (1936). "An Unsolvable Problem of Elementary Number Theory."
*American Journal of Mathematics*, 58(2), 345--363.

## Summary

This landmark paper contains:

1. The proof that the equivalence of lambda expressions is in general not decidable.

2. The first explicit formulation of Church's thesis (Section 7):
   "We now define the notion, already discussed, of an effectively calculable
   function of positive integers by identifying it with the notion of a recursive
   function of positive integers (or of a lambda-definable function of positive
   integers)."

3. A negative solution to the Entscheidungsproblem (decision problem) for
   first-order logic, showing that there is no effective procedure that can
   determine, for every statement in first-order logic, whether it is provable.

4. The demonstration that lambda-definable functions coincide with the general
   recursive functions (building on work with Kleene).

## Significance

This paper, together with Turing's 1936 paper "On Computable Numbers," established
the foundations of computability theory. The equivalence of Church's lambda-definability
and Turing's machine-computability (shown by Turing) provided strong evidence for
the Church-Turing thesis.
