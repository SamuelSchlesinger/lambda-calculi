# Barendregt -- "The Lambda Calculus: Its Syntax and Semantics"

## Citation

Barendregt, H.P. (1984). *The Lambda Calculus: Its Syntax and Semantics*.
Revised edition. Studies in Logic and the Foundations of Mathematics, Volume 103.
North-Holland (Elsevier). ISBN: 0-444-87508-5.

Also reprinted by College Publications (2012), ISBN: 978-1-84890-066-0.

## Overview

This is the definitive reference monograph on the untyped lambda calculus. The book
treats the lambda calculus mainly in its untyped version, consisting of a collection
of expressions called lambda terms, together with ways to rewrite and identify these.

## Structure

The book is organized into five main parts:

1. **Introduction** -- Basic definitions and notation
2. **Conversion** -- The algebraic view: alpha, beta, eta equivalence
3. **Reduction** -- The computational view: reduction strategies, normalization,
   Church-Rosser theorem, standardization
4. **Theories** -- The coinductive view: lambda theories, Bohm trees,
   sensible theories
5. **Models** -- The set-theoretic view: Scott domains, D-infinity models,
   graph models, categorical models

Additional chapters cover:
- Classical lambda calculus (combinatory logic)
- The theory of combinators

Appendices include:
- The pure typed lambda calculus
- Formulae-as-types (Curry-Howard correspondence)
- Illative combinatory logic

## Significance

This book remains the standard reference for the mathematical theory of the
untyped lambda calculus. It contains comprehensive treatments of:
- Bohm's theorem and separability
- Solvability and head normal forms
- The standardization theorem
- Scott's D-infinity models and other semantic models
- Lambda definability
- The connection between lambda calculus and combinatory logic
